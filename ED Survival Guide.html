EM survival guide HEENT

Ear

Sx: otalgia, sudden hearing loss, discharge and debris, vertigo, N/V, headache, fever, trauma to the ear, recent URI.

Signs:prominent blood vessels on tympanic membrane (otitis media), dix-Hallpike test, pain on moving the penna, tympanic membrane perforation, and foreign bodies in the ear, bulla on TM.


Otitis Externa

  • Caused by P. aeruginosa (Most common), enterobacteriaceae, proteus, staph. otomycosis (tropical climates and immunocompromised, long term Abx). Aspergillous and candida most common fungal pathogens.
  • Rx: Ofloxacin otic 5 drops BID, acetic acid/hydrocortisone otic 5 drops TID (not with perforated TM), ciprofloxacin/hydrocortisone otic 3 drops BID for 7 days.

Maligant Otitis Externa

  • 90% caused by P. aeruginosa, in elderly, DM, immunocompromised pt. CT is necessary to determin the extent and stage of disease.
  • Rx: Tobramycin 2 mg/kg IV and pipracillin 3-4 gm IV or ceftriaxone 1 GM IV OR Ciprofloxacin 400 MG IV + Admit.

Otitis Media

Causes

  • acute: S. pneumoniae (48%), H. influenza (29%), and M. catarrhalis (28%).
  • Chronic: S. aureus, P. aeruginosa, anaerobes.

Complications

perforation of TM, conductive hearing loss, acute serous labyrinthiitis, facial nerve paralysis, mastoiditis, lateral sinus thrombosis, cholesteatoma and intracranial complications.

Rx

  • Pain control

    • Tylenol/Ibuprofen (>6 months old)

  • Abx

< 6months

antibiotics even if uncertain diagnosis

6 months-2 years

antibiotics if diagnosis is certain or if uncertain but febrile to 39 C or moderate to severe otalgia

>6 months

with uncertain diagnosis may be treated symptomatically while deferring antibiotics for 48-72 hours. Close follow up with PCP.

Antibiotic options

  • 10 days of Amoxicillin 250-500 mg PO TID, or Azithromycin 500 mg PO OD for 1 day then 250 mg PO OD for 4 days, or cefuroxime 500 mg PO BID for 10 d.
  • benign allergic reaction

    • Penicillin allergic-Cefdinir, cefuroxime, cefpodoxime

  • Anaphylaxis to penicillin

    • Azithromycin, Clarithromycin, Bacterium.

  • If unresponsive after 72 hours Cefuroxime or amoxicillin/ clavulanate.
  • If OM with effusion extend treatment to 3 weeks.

Acute Mastoiditis

  • CT to further characterize extent of boney involvement.
  • Rx: Vancomycin 1-2 gm IV orceftriaxone 1 gm IV admission and possible surgical drainage.

Lateral Sinus Thrombosis

  • Dx can be made with CT although MRI or angiography may be necessary.
  • Rx: Nafcillin 2 gm IV, Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV and Metronidazole 500 mg IV and admission.

Bullous Myringitis

  • Causes: viruses, M. Pneumoniae, chlamydia psittaci.
  • Rx: symptomatic with pain control Abx if concomitant OM.

Trauma to the ear

  • if hematoma immediate I&D with compressive dressing to prevent cauliflower ear.
  • Thermal injuries Rx with cleaning and topical non sulfa containing Abx ointment and light dressing.
  • Frostbite Rx with rapid rewarming by using saline soaked gauze at 38C-40C. Give analgesics before rewarming.

Foreign Bodies in the ear

  • Look for signs of infection or perforation.
  • Live insects 2% lidocaine solution distilled into the ear canal.
  • Can be removed with direct forceps or irrigation hooked probe or suction cath.
  • Consult to ENT if TM perforated.

TM perforation

Abx are not necessary unless there is persistent foreign material in the canal or middle ear.
